# Jewish Kahals of Kijów Voivodeship, 1765

## Overview

A kahal-level population dataset of the Jewish communities in the **Kijów Voivodeship** (Województwo Kijowskie), compiled from the **1765 Jewish Poll Tax Census** (*Pogłówne Żydowskie*). The census was mandated by the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth's Convocation Sejm of 1764, which imposed a per-capita tax of 2 złoty on every Jewish man, woman, and child. Local commissioners visited each kahal, recorded every household by name, and tallied the population.

## Coverage

The dataset covers the Kijów Voivodeship in the eastern Polish Crown (modern-day central Ukraine), encompassing **72 kahals**, **1,145 settlements**, and a total of **25,872 Jewish individuals**:

- **Urban population** — 16,983 Jews living in the principal kahal towns
- **Rural population** — 8,889 Jews dispersed across surrounding villages, taverns, and estates

The voivodeship included three powiats: Kijów, Żytomierz, and Owrucz.

## Layers

**Districts** — Polygonal boundaries of the 72 kahal districts, each recording the kahal name, Hebrew name, urban/rural/total population, and number of subsidiary places.

**Points** — 1,145 individual settlements (towns, villages, estates) geocoded to their modern locations, with kahal affiliation and population.

## Source

Based on the publication by Kałczinski and Kłuczycki (KLC), which summarized the results of the 1765 Jewish Poll Tax Census. Geographic identification and geocoding performed for the present dataset.

## Timeframe

Census year: **1765**.
