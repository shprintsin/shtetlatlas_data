# Jewish Kahals of Rus Voivodeship, 1765

## Overview

A kahal-level population dataset of the Jewish communities in the **Rus Voivodeship** (Województwo Ruskie), compiled from the **1765 Jewish Poll Tax Census** (*Pogłówne Żydowskie*). The census was mandated by the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth's Convocation Sejm of 1764, which imposed a per-capita tax of 2 złoty on every Jewish man, woman, and child. Local commissioners visited each kahal, recorded every household by name, and tallied the population.

## Coverage

The dataset covers the Rus Voivodeship in the southeastern Polish Crown (modern-day western Ukraine), encompassing **108 kahals**, **1,491 settlements**, and a total of **81,705 Jewish individuals**:

- **Urban population** — 65,885 Jews living in the principal kahal towns
- **Rural population** — 15,820 Jews dispersed across surrounding villages, taverns, and estates

The voivodeship included powiats: Lwów, Halicz, Kołomyja, Trembowla, and Żydaczew.

## Layers

**Districts** — Polygonal boundaries of the 108 kahal districts, each recording the kahal name, Hebrew name, urban/rural/total population, and number of subsidiary places.

**Points** — 1,491 individual settlements (towns, villages, estates) geocoded to their modern locations, with kahal affiliation and population.

## Source

Based on the publication by Kałczinski and Kłuczycki (KLC), which summarized the results of the 1765 Jewish Poll Tax Census. Geographic identification and geocoding performed for the present dataset.

## Timeframe

Census year: **1765**.
